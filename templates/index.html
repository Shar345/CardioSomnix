Technology Use and Sleep

India has one of the highest rates of technology use before bed, with over 60% of Indians using mobile devices late at night. This contributes to poor sleep quality, elevating the risk of cardiovascular disease, a problem also seen globally.

Learn More

India and Sleep Deprivation

Compared to global averages, India ranks among the top countries with sleep deprivation issues. Studies indicate that 55% of Indians report not getting enough sleep, which increases the risk of cardiovascular diseases like hypertension and stroke.

Learn More

Cardiovascular Mortality Rates

India accounts for 20% of global cardiovascular deaths, with rising sleep disorders such as sleep apnea and insomnia. Increasing stress, urbanization, and lifestyle changes contribute to these disorders, significantly raising the risk of heart failure and arrhythmias.

Learn More

India vs. Developed Countries

Indians, on average, sleep about 7.1 hours per night, slightly less than people in developed countries such as the U.S. or the U.K., where average sleep is closer to 7.5 hours. This sleep deficit raises the risk of heart-related conditions, especially in urban areas.

Sleep Chronotypes

Discover Your Animal Sleep Chronotype

Based on your sleep patterns, you can be categorized into specific sleep chronotypes represented by animals. Find out which animal reflects your sleep habits and how to optimize your daily routine.

🐻
Bear Chronotype

Characteristics: Sleep aligns with the sun, productive mid-day, generally needs 8 hours of sleep.

Cardiovascular Risks: Moderate risk; generally balanced sleep but prolonged stress or inconsistent patterns may increase risk.

Read More
🦁
Lion Chronotype

Characteristics: Early riser, productive in morning, winds down early, consistent sleep routine.

Cardiovascular Risks: Lower risk due to regular sleep patterns, active lifestyle, and morning exercise.

Read More
🐺
Wolf Chronotype

Characteristics: Night-oriented, energetic in late afternoon /evening, struggles with mornings.

Cardiovascular Risks: Higher risk due to irregular sleep, late-night eating, low daytime activity, and increased blood pressure.

Read More
🐬
Dolphin Chronotype

Characteristics: Light sleeper, irregular sleep patterns, anxious, frequently wakes during the night.

Cardiovascular Risks: Higher risk due to poor sleep quality, chronic sleep deprivation, and stress leading to hypertension.
